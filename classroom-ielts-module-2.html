Article / Lesson Notes

IELTS Listening moves from social context (Section 1) to academic complexity (Section 4). Your core goal is not to catch every word, but to capture meaning, transitions, and answer signals. Listen for cue language like “actually,” “however,” and “the main point is…” because test writers often place distractors before the final answer.

  • Section strategy: predict likely word type (number, noun, name) before audio starts.
  • Trap control: when you hear a correction ("it was Tuesday—sorry, I mean Thursday"), use the corrected version.
  • Answer transfer: keep spelling and plural forms accurate to avoid losing easy marks.

Do

  • Read questions quickly before each section begins.
  • Use abbreviations in notes (e.g., env = environment).
  • Review wrong answers by trap type after each drill.

Don’t

  • Freeze on one missed answer and lose the next two.
  • Write full sentences while audio is playing.
  • Ignore grammar clues in gap-fill tasks.
